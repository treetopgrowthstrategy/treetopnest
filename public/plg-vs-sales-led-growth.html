PLG vs Sales-Led Growth: How to Choose the Right Motion

Choosing between PLG and sales-led isn't a philosophical decision — it's an operational one driven by your ACV, buyer profile, and time-to-value. Here's the framework.

By Bill Colbert, founder of Treetop Growth Strategy · Updated June 2026

The PLG vs sales-led debate has generated more conference talks and LinkedIn posts than almost any other B2B topic in the last five years. Most of the debate is philosophical. This guide is operational: given your specific product, ACV, buyer, and stage, which motion produces better unit economics?

The Decision Framework

Answer these five questions before picking a motion:

  1. What is your current ACV? PLG is economically viable below $5K ACV. Sales-led is required above $25K. Between $5K-$25K, you have a genuine choice — evaluate the other factors.
  2. Who makes the buying decision? If an individual contributor can adopt the product without organizational approval, PLG is feasible. If the decision requires a committee, budget approval, or procurement, you need a sales motion regardless of ACV.
  3. How fast does the product deliver value? PLG requires short time-to-value. If a user needs 4+ hours of setup before seeing value, PLG activation rates will be too low to be economically viable.
  4. Do you have product analytics infrastructure? PLG only works with deep instrumentation. If you can't track activation events, feature adoption, and expansion signals by user, you can't run PLG effectively.
  5. What does your competitive set look like? If the market leader runs PLG and you run sales-led, you're playing a structural disadvantage in attracting individual users who self-select into free tiers. If the market leader is sales-led and you can run PLG, you have a significant CAC advantage.

Hybrid Motions: The Reality for Most $5M-$20M ARR Companies

Most companies operating between $5M-$20M ARR run a hybrid motion whether they call it that or not. PLG drives SMB and mid-market adoption through self-serve; a sales team works enterprise accounts and manages PLG-to-enterprise expansion. The challenge with hybrid is that it requires two separate playbooks, two different sets of metrics, and organizational clarity about which motion applies to which segment.

The common failure mode: a company optimizes its PLG motion for SMB at the expense of building the enterprise sales muscle it needs to capture the large accounts that will drive the majority of ARR. Enterprise sales in a PLG company requires specific skills — patience, multi-stakeholder navigation, and comfort with long sales cycles — that are different from the optimization mindset that drives PLG growth.

When to Switch Motions

PLG-to-sales-led: When enterprise accounts start showing up in your user base and you're losing them to competitors who have sales teams to manage the relationship. The signal is large companies starting trials and then churning without converting — not because the product isn't good, but because nobody called them.

Sales-led-to-PLG: When your product has become self-serve intuitive enough that a free tier would reduce CAC substantially and your per-seat economics support a bottom-up motion. This rarely happens by accident — it requires product investment to reduce the friction that currently requires sales.

What is product-led growth?

Product-led growth (PLG) is a GTM motion where the product itself drives acquisition, activation, and expansion — with minimal or no sales involvement in the initial adoption. PLG relies on free or freemium access, short time-to-value, and self-serve onboarding. Classic PLG companies include Slack, Figma, Notion, and Dropbox.

What is sales-led growth?

Sales-led growth is a GTM motion where a sales team converts inbound and outbound leads into customers. The sales team manages the relationship from first contact through close and often owns the expansion conversation. Sales-led growth is the dominant model for high-ACV B2B products where the buying decision involves multiple stakeholders.

Can a company run both PLG and sales-led?

Yes. Most companies between $5M-$20M ARR run some form of hybrid — PLG for SMB and self-serve segments, sales-led for enterprise accounts. The key to making hybrid work is organizational clarity about which motion applies to which segment and separate playbooks for each.
