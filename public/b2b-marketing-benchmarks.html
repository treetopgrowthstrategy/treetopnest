B2B Marketing Benchmarks 2026: Email, Pipeline, Content, and Paid

You can't set meaningful 90-day targets without knowing what good looks like. This is the benchmark reference for every major B2B marketing metric — with annotations on what actually moves each one.

By Bill Colbert, founder of Treetop Growth Strategy · Updated June 2026

Setting meaningful 90-day goals requires knowing what good looks like in your category before you set a single target. This is the benchmark reference for B2B marketing leaders — organized by function and annotated with what moves each metric and what doesn't.

How to use this:

Pull your current numbers first. Then find where you sit in each table. Your 30-day goal should be to understand why you're where you are. Your 60-day goal should be to move the metrics where intervention is highest leverage. Your 90-day goal should be to show measurable delta from baseline on your top three metrics.

What moves open rate: Subject line testing is the highest-leverage action — a 15% lift in open rate is achievable in 30-45 days through systematic subject line A/B testing. Send-time optimization adds 2-4 pts. List hygiene (removing unengaged subscribers) often improves open rate immediately but reduces volume — worth it if your goal is deliverability.

What doesn't move open rate fast: Content quality. Open rate is decided before the email is read. Focus on subject lines and preview text, not the body copy, if open rate is your primary problem.

How to Set 30-Day Targets From Your Baseline

The right methodology: identify which two or three metrics are furthest below benchmark, understand why (audit the inputs, not just the outputs), and set a 30-day target that's achievable through intervention rather than hoping. A 5-point improvement in email open rate is achievable in 30 days through subject line testing. A 10-point improvement in MQL-to-SQL requires a definition conversation with sales — that's also achievable in 30 days. A 50% increase in marketing-sourced pipeline is not achievable in 30 days without a windfall of luck. Set targets you can actually trace to a mechanism.

What are average B2B email open rates?

B2B email open rates average 20-25%. Good is 28-35%. Best-in-class campaigns regularly exceed 40%. Open rate is primarily determined by subject line, sender name, and list hygiene — not email body content.

What is a good lead-to-MQL conversion rate?

The industry average for lead-to-MQL conversion is 10-15%. Good is 20-30%. If your rate is below 8%, the most common cause is a loosely defined MQL threshold — tightening the scoring criteria with your sales team typically produces a faster improvement than improving lead quality.

What is a good pipeline coverage ratio?

A healthy pipeline coverage ratio is 3x your quota. 4x is good. Below 2.5x indicates pipeline risk — if close rates come in below projection, there isn't enough backup pipeline to make the number. Higher ACV businesses typically require more coverage because their deal count is lower.

What is B2B marketing-sourced pipeline percentage?

B2B companies average 30-40% marketing-sourced pipeline. Good is 50-60%. Product-led growth companies often exceed 70%. If marketing-sourced pipeline is below 20%, it usually indicates the company has historically been sales-led with minimal demand gen investment.

What is a good CAC payback period for SaaS?

The SaaS industry average CAC payback period is 15-18 months. Under 12 months is strong. Under 6 months is world-class, typically seen in PLG companies with high product virality. Over 24 months indicates a unit economics problem that marketing changes alone may not solve.
