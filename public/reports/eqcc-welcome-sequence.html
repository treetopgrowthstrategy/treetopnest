Email 02 — The Mountain
1,400 meters. One valley. Everything changes here.
Send Day 2
Preview Text Most coffee tells you the country. We'll tell you the altitude.

Matagalpa sits at the center of Nicaragua like a secret.

The capital doesn't talk about it much. The travel guides mention it briefly. But the farmers who work this land know what visitors rarely do — that the elevation here does something to coffee that you can't replicate anywhere else.

At 1,400 meters, the nights turn cold even when the days are warm. That temperature swing forces the coffee cherry to develop slowly. The sugars concentrate. The acids sharpen and then soften into something more complex than the word "bright" can hold.

The volcanic soil underneath has been building for centuries. It doesn't need fertilizer so much as respect.

Specific detail about the farm land — acreage, how long the family or producers have worked it, what makes this particular terroir distinct from other Nicaraguan growing regions. A sensory detail or two would land well here.

This is why we don't source from multiple regions and call it a collection. We source from one place, deeply, and let the varietals tell the rest of the story.

Six lots. One mountain. Everything else is just elevation.

Email 03 — The Lots
Six coffees. Each one a different answer.
Send Day 5
Preview Text The Geisha is not the Anaerobic Java. Both are worth knowing.

People sometimes ask us which coffee is our best.

We don't have a good answer to that. It's the wrong question — like asking which season is the most important one.

Here is what we grow:

Strictly High Grown (SHG) — Washed

Clean. Honest. The one you come back to. Scored 84 points, but points miss the point. This is the coffee that holds a morning together.

Java — Washed

86 points. A longer story in the cup. The Java variety arrived in Nicaragua through a winding path, and it remembers the journey. Complex without being difficult.

Anaerobic Java

The same variety, a completely different conversation. Anaerobic processing turns the volume up. Fruit-forward, ferment-edged, the kind of coffee that divides a cupping table in the best possible way.

Dwarf Honey Java

Processed between washed and natural. The honey method catches the sweetness before it fully separates. A gentler version of the Java story.

Yellow Catuai

Catuai is a workhorse variety, but ours doesn't act like one. The yellow cherry brings a softer sweetness than red. Less demanding, deeply satisfying.

Geisha

We don't need to oversell this one. You already know the name. What we'll say is this: ours earns it.

One sentence per lot with any specific harvest detail, process story, or producer note that makes each feel personal. Even small details — a drying elevation, a fermentation time, a particular farmer's name — add weight here.

Email 04 — The People
The farm has a name. So does everyone who works it.
Send Day 9
Preview Text Coffee is grown by people. We think you should know theirs.

The bag doesn't tell you enough.

It tells you the origin, the process, the score. What it can't tell you is what it looked like the morning the cherry was picked — the mist still sitting low in the valley, the pickers moving through rows of red and yellow, the sound of the de-pulper starting up.

Introduce the producers or farmers by name. Their story — how long they've farmed, their family connection to the land, one specific detail that makes them human and real. This is the emotional core of the entire sequence. It should feel like an introduction, not a credential.

We source directly because we believe the relationship is part of the product.

When you buy a bag — or when you buy a lot — some of that money goes directly back to the people who grew it. Not through three intermediaries and a co-op spreadsheet. Directly.

Specific direct-trade or pricing detail that demonstrates the commitment — what percentage above commodity price, how the relationship was built over time, or a moment that cemented the partnership.

We think coffee tastes better when you know this.

Email 05 — The Invitation
This is where it becomes yours.
Send Day 14
Preview Text You've seen the mountain. Now we want to hear from you.

You've been with us for a few weeks now.

You know the mountain. You know the lots. You know a little about why we do this — the elevation, the soil, the people, the slow work of getting something right.

Now we want to know about you.

If you're a roaster: what are you building? What hole in your menu are you trying to fill? Is it the workhorse espresso that holds the whole program together, or the seasonal natural that gives your regulars something to talk about? We have opinions. We'd like to share them.

If you're a coffee drinker: what does a great morning mean to you? What are you looking for in a cup that you haven't quite found yet? Tell us. We'll point you somewhere specific.

Either way — the quiz takes 60 seconds, and at the end of it, we'll tell you exactly which lot we think was grown for you. Not generically. Specifically.

Optional — personal closing note from Justin. Even two or three sentences in his own voice makes this feel like a letter, not a campaign. Sign it as him.

This is where the relationship starts. We're glad you're here.

Execution Notes

  • Send Day 0, 2, 5, 9, 14. The sequence should breathe — not feel rushed.
  • Voice throughout: unhurried, literary, grounded in place. No urgency language. No discounts. No "act now."
  • Email 04 (The People) is the emotional peak. It should not send until the story insert is filled in with real names and real details.
  • The quiz CTA is introduced early (Email 01) but earns its primary push in Email 05. That's intentional.
  • All emails work for both roaster and consumer audiences without branching. The language is layered enough to serve both.
  • Story inserts are flagged in amber throughout. These are the only items requiring client input before the sequence goes live.
