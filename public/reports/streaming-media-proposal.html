The opportunity

Why streaming now — and why it compounds

Your brand television opportunity is at an inflection point. Three forces have changed simultaneously: streaming has surpassed linear TV in time-spent for every demo under 50, ad-supported tiers on Hulu and Amazon have reached critical audience mass, and first-party targeting on these platforms is now more precise than anything cable ever offered.

The window is real. Early entrants in your category are locking in audience relationships and building branded search lift before CPMs spike as category demand increases. This is a 12–18 month window before streaming ad costs normalize to linear TV levels.

The compounding effect: Streaming TV builds branded search volume — people who see your ad on Hulu search for you on Google within 72 hours at measurable rates. This means your streaming spend lifts your SEO/SEM efficiency simultaneously, creating a multiplier that doesn't exist in any other channel.

Creative production New

What it costs to be on air — and how to do it right

Creative is the most common blocker at activation. The answer is to plan production in parallel with media setup so we're not waiting on creative when the buy is ready to launch.

Production tier What you get Est. cost Timeline Best for
Efficient launch 1 x :30 spot, existing brand assets repurposed with professional VO and edit $8K–$15K 2–3 weeks Testing phase — prove the channel before full spend
Standard broadcast 1 x :30 + 1 x :15, original shoot, 2 talent, 1 location, 2 revision rounds $20K–$35K 4–6 weeks Phase 1 full launch — Hulu-ready, brand story lead
Full A/B package 2 x :30 variants (brand story + DR version) + :15 cut-downs, same shoot day $35K–$55K 5–7 weeks Recommended — amortizes production cost, enables testing
Recommended approach: Produce two creative variants on a single shoot day — an emotional brand story version and a direct response CTA version. Same talent, same locations, different scripts and edits. This doubles your testing surface without doubling production cost, and gives us the data to know which message converts within 4–6 weeks of being on air.
Production is passed through at cost. Treetop does not mark up production. We manage the process and recommend partners, but the invoice goes directly from production company to client. No margin on production is a standard term of our engagement.

Measurement & attribution Expanded

How we know if it's working — at every stage

CTV attribution is more complex than paid social because there is no click. Here is the three-pronged model that gives us actionable data within 2 weeks of a flight going live.

Signal 1
Branded search lift
We measure Google and Amazon branded search volume in markets where we're running vs. holdout markets where we're dark. A 15–20% lift in flight markets within 48–72 hours of campaign start is the clearest signal CTV is driving awareness. Tracked weekly.
Signal 2
Amazon dual CTA tracking
QR code scans provide immediate, attributable conversion data for mobile second-screen viewers. Vanity URL creates a unique search term we can isolate in Search Console. Together these give us the most direct CTV attribution available in any platform.
Signal 3
Holdout market methodology
We designate 2–3 comparable markets where we don't run and use them as a control. Direct traffic, organic conversions, and branded search in holdout vs. flight markets gives us a statistically valid read on incremental lift.
Decision framework
Kill criteria & scale triggers
Week 6 decision gate: if branded search lift is below 15% and CPMs are running 25%+ above model, we pause and diagnose. If we're hitting benchmarks, we increase budget. No ambiguity about what constitutes success — we agree on the numbers before we launch.
