Section 01

What Fractional GTM actually means

What you get

A senior GTM operator embedded in your business at a fraction of the cost of a full-time VP of Growth or Head of Sales. Running the functions that drive acquisition: identifying who to sell to, validating that they're real and reachable, building the outreach infrastructure, and running paid social to capture inbound demand in parallel.

What you don't get

An agency that reports on vanity metrics. A junior SDR sending bulk emails. A consultant who delivers a strategy deck and disappears. Separate invoices for strategy, execution, and reporting. Work that stops when you stop paying attention to it.

Most B2B companies don't have a pipeline problem. They have a targeting problem, they're reaching the wrong companies, too early, with the wrong message. The GTM work starts with getting that right before a single outreach email is sent.
Section 02

The two service lines

Service line 01
B2B Acquisition Engine
Prospecting, validation, and outreach, end to end.
What this covers
  • ICP definition and refinement: building the firmographic and behavioral profile of your best-fit buyer. Not a brainstorm, a validated framework based on your existing customer data and win/loss patterns.
  • Prospect research and list building: identifying real companies that match the ICP across company size, industry, geography, tech stack, hiring signals, and funding status. Built manually and via tooling, not scraped lists.
  • Target validation: every prospect qualified against defined criteria before entering a sequence. No spray-and-pray. Effort concentrated on accounts most likely to convert.
  • Outreach sequence build: personalized multi-touch sequences across email and LinkedIn. Written to reflect the prospect's actual context, not a template with a first name field.
  • Sequence management and optimization: A/B testing subject lines, messaging, timing, and CTAs. Response handling and reply triage. Sequence rotation when fatigue sets in.
  • Weekly and monthly reporting: prospects contacted, response rates by sequence, meetings booked, pipeline generated. No attribution games, straightforward numbers.
Service line 02
Meta Ads Management
Paid social acquisition. Setup, optimization, and reporting.
What this covers
  • Campaign architecture: account structure, campaign objective selection, ad set configuration, audience segmentation. Built for performance from day one, not retrofitted later.
  • Audience management: pixel retargeting, lookalike builds from customer seed data, suppression lists, and ongoing audience refresh as data accumulates.
  • Creative testing framework: systematic A/B testing of creative concepts, copy angles, CTAs, and formats. Rotation cadence set by performance data, not a calendar.
  • Bid strategy and budget optimization: daily monitoring, budget pacing, CPM and CPC management. Rebalancing between ad sets and campaigns based on real-time performance.
  • Weekly performance report: spend, impressions, CPM, CTR, ROAS, CAC trend. Delivered every Monday.
  • Monthly strategy report: full-funnel attribution, creative winner analysis, audience performance breakdown, recommended budget adjustments for the following month.
Section 03

The work: what actually happens

Phase 01
Define
Week 1 to 2. Before a single prospect is contacted or a single dollar is spent.
  • ICP workshop: 90-minute session to map your best-fit buyer profile against existing customer data
  • Win/loss analysis: which deals closed, which churned, and why
  • Message architecture: one primary value proposition per ICP, three supporting proof points, one differentiated claim competitors cannot make
  • Competitive landscape: who else is in your buyer's consideration set and how to position against them
  • Approval gate: ICP profile and message architecture signed off before any outreach begins
Phase 02
Build
Weeks 2 to 4. Infrastructure before execution.
  • Prospect list build: first batch of 100 to 300 validated targets matching approved ICP
  • Sequence build: three-touch to five-touch outreach sequence per ICP, written and reviewed
  • Technical setup: email domain warm-up, LinkedIn profile optimization, CRM integration, tracking infrastructure
  • Meta account setup: campaign structure, pixel verification, audience seeds uploaded, first creative batch QA'd
  • Pre-launch review: all assets reviewed and approved before any outreach or spend is activated
Phase 03
Run
Month 2 onward. Execution with continuous optimization.
  • Weekly outreach: new prospects entering sequences continuously against monthly volume targets
  • Response management: all replies triaged, interested prospects handed off or advanced to next step
  • Meta optimization: daily monitoring, weekly creative and bid adjustments, monthly budget rebalancing
  • Weekly reporting: outreach metrics plus Meta performance in a single combined report
  • Monthly call: 60-minute strategy review, optimization decisions, next month planning

Section 05

How we measure the work

Measurement 01
B2B Outbound

Primary metrics: prospects contacted per month vs. target, response rate (benchmark 8 to 15% for well-targeted outbound), positive response rate (benchmark 2 to 5%), meetings booked per month, pipeline value generated.

Secondary: sequence reply-rate by touch number, best-performing message variant, ICP accuracy signal (positive vs. negative response patterns).

What we don't measure: open rates (unreliable), impressions, or any metric that doesn't connect to pipeline.

Measurement 02
Meta Ads

Primary metrics: spend vs. budget pacing, CPM, CTR, CPC, ROAS (revenue/spend), CAC (cost to acquire one customer).

Secondary: creative performance by variant, audience overlap, frequency, and reach by campaign.

Reporting cadence: weekly summary every Monday, monthly full-attribution report by the 5th.

Measurement 03
Combined

North Star: cost per qualified meeting booked (outbound) and cost per acquisition (Meta).

Monthly review question: Is the blended CAC trending toward the client's target? If yes, scale. If no, diagnose which input (ICP accuracy, message quality, creative, audience targeting) is causing the gap before adding budget.

Section 06

What we need from you

Before we start

  • 90 minutes for an ICP and messaging workshop in week one. Non-negotiable, the work can't begin without it.
  • Access to CRM or deal history (even a spreadsheet) so we can analyze existing customers before building new target lists
  • 2 to 3 case studies, testimonials, or customer proof points we can use in outreach messaging
  • Designated point of contact with authority to approve ICP definition, messaging, and sequence copy before launch
  • Meta Business Manager access and pixel confirmed on website (or we set it up in week one)

During the engagement

  • Respond to ICP, messaging, and creative approvals within 3 business days
  • Weekly 15-minute sync (optional but recommended). These keep the work from drifting.
  • Flag any product changes, pricing changes, or target market shifts immediately. These break sequences if we don't know about them.
The single biggest engagement failure mode is approval bottlenecks. Sequences can't go live without approved copy. Meta campaigns can't launch without approved creative. The faster approvals move on your end, the faster the engine runs.
