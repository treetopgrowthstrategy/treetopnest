The Move · Filters

Striking-heavy or MMA-blended. BJJ-only schools are deprioritized. The gear catalog is gloves, headgear, shin guards, hand wraps. Pure grappling schools do not consume that gear.

02
Operating Model

Membership-based with progression curriculum. Drop-in studios and class-pass models are deprioritized. Recurring members generate the replacement cycle that funds the channel.

Brand Authority

Affiliated with a recognized lineage, fighter, or method (ATT, Tiger Schulmann, Gracie Barra, 10th Planet). Brand-name gyms make brand-name gear partnerships work.

300+ active members. Average monthly dues $150+. This is the threshold where the activation math clears $25K/yr per gym. Below this, the unit economics break.

The Mechanic

FIVE MOTIONS INSIDE A PARTNER GYM

Signing the gym is not enough. Gyms forget to sell. The activation playbook converts the partnership into recurring revenue through five member-moments each year.

01

Welcome Kit · Onboarding Moment

Every new member receives an ONX welcome kit (hand wraps, basic gear) in their first month. Gym buys at wholesale, builds it into membership pricing. Predictable unit per new member.

02

Sparring Bundle · Class Progression Trigger

When a member moves from cardio to sparring class, headgear and shin guards become mandatory. Gym presents the "Sparring-Ready Bundle" at ~$400. A real merchandising moment.

03

Quarterly Refresh · Replacement Cycle

Gloves wear in 6 to 12 months. Structured refresh promotion: trade in old gloves for $50 off new ones. Repeatable. Predictable.

04

Sponsored Fighter · Fight-Night Moment

The gym's amateurs and pros wear ONX at every local card. Photographed, broadcast, posted on gym social. Built-in replacement cycle from fight wear.

05

Wittman Seminar · Annual Event

Once per year per top partner. Half-day Wittman seminar at the gym. Members pay $150 to attend. Gear sells at the event. Gym keeps the event revenue.

Hayabusa and Venum can sell gloves to a gym. They cannot send Trevor Wittman to coach a seminar at the gym. The hardest-to-replicate offering in the competitive set is sitting on the ONX founder bio.

Fifty partners in Year One. Two hundred in Year Two.

The Mechanic · Pre-Signing

SIX SIGNALS BEFORE A SIGNATURE

Most evaluation watches the wrong metrics. Follower count, total views, engagement rate predict almost nothing about purchase intent.

01
Audience Quality

HypeAuditor verification. >85% real followers. Catches the "1M followers, 60% bots" trap.

02
Audience-Product Fit

Percent who actually train. 500K with 60% practitioners beats 2M with 15%.

03
Earned Media

In the press cycle monthly? Sherdog, Fightmatrix, Yahoo Sports. Earned media multiplies the partnership for free.

04
Endorsement Load

Lower is better. Five existing brands = 1/5 share of voice. One or two = real visibility.

05
Storytelling Capacity

Technique posts vs lifestyle posts. Pereira posts technique. Most post the gym mirror.

06
Rising Indicator

Recent contract advancement: new UFC deal, championship win, breakout fight. The rising athlete signs cheap.

The Mechanic · Measurement

90-DAY REVENUE PER FOLLOWER (RPF)

Stop measuring views. Measure RPF: total attributed revenue divided by total followers. Every athlete sorts into a bucket every 90 days. Below threshold for two windows = awareness-only or cut.

Tier RPF Threshold Action
Anchor >$0.30 Keep, expand, consider exclusivity
Rising Star >$0.50 Keep, expand budget
Emerging / Local Pro >$0.75 Keep, possibly elevate
Amateur Pipeline >$1.00 Keep, possibly elevate

Higher tiers have lower thresholds because larger audiences dilute. Smaller athletes with concentrated practitioner audiences often outperform on RPF. Both are legitimate. The threshold structure accounts for the asymmetry.

The Mechanic · Compensation

THE COMP STRUCTURE THAT CHANGES BEHAVIOR

Base Retainer
30% of total comp

Predictable monthly. Covers content delivery, exclusivity. Smaller than industry standard. That is the point.

Performance Commission
60% of total comp

5–15% commission on attributed sales via unique link and unique promo code. Athletes make more by driving sales.

Equity (Anchor Only)
10% of total comp value, 4-year vest

For Gaethje-tier signings. 0.1–0.25% equity aligns long-term incentive.

Athletes who know they make more by driving sales post differently. They post the gear, not the gym mirror. They include the code, not just the brand name. They become a sales channel, not a billboard.

The Mechanic

PAID + ORGANIC, ALWAYS PAIRED

Every paid keyword cluster maps to an organic content piece. "Best gloves for sparring" runs alongside "Best Sparring Gloves by Training Level." Paid validates the query intent and drives short-term sales. Organic captures the long-term lift.

Hayabusa spends $1M annually on paid and gets 1,800 monthly paid visitors. ONX at $30K-$50K monthly, weighted to long-tail and brand protection, targets 800-1,200 monthly paid visitors with significantly higher intent. The economics work because we are not competing for "boxing gloves." We own queries Hayabusa is not bidding on.
