04 — Competitive Gaps
Where Pro Air Tech Is Exposed
No maintenance membership
All four rivals use monthly plans ($16.99–higher) to lock in recurring revenue and reduce churn. Ragsdale at $16.99/mo, Precision's MVP club at 10% discount hook. Pro Air Tech has no visible program — leaving ARR and LTV on the table.
HVAC-only service scope
Three of four rivals bundle plumbing and/or electrical. This increases average ticket size and eliminates the need for a customer to call a second company — reducing churn and increasing lifetime value per household.
Thin review volume
Competitors have 608–30,000+ Google reviews. Low review count directly suppresses Google LSA ranking (which weighs star rating and review volume heavily) and reduces consumer trust at the zero moment of truth.
No promotions / coupons page
Ragsdale and Precision run active specials pages with seasonal coupons and financing offers. These pages give paid traffic a reason to convert and generate repeat organic visits from price-conscious homeowners.
No geo-specific landing pages
Zero Hiram, Dallas, or Paulding County pages exist. Competitors have built and indexed these pages — they rank organically and serve as high-Quality Score landing pages for paid campaigns simultaneously.
Social media absent
Precision actively uses Facebook, Instagram, and LinkedIn. Casteel runs flash sales and community posts. Pro Air Tech has no linked social profiles on its website — missing brand awareness and remarketing audience building.
No manufacturer certification
Ragsdale holds Carrier's President's Award (highest dealer honor) and Precision is a Trane Comfort Specialist. Both unlock co-op advertising funds from manufacturers — effectively subsidizing competitor ad budgets that Pro Air Tech must match at full cost.
No blog or content marketing
No visible blog, seasonal tips, or educational content. Competitors produce HVAC maintenance guides and seasonal articles that capture long-tail organic search traffic — visitors who then convert through remarketing.
05 — Prioritized Action Plan
What Pro Air Tech Should Do First
Sequenced by ROI speed and strategic dependency
  • 1
    Activate Google Local Services Ads (LSA) Immediate
    LSAs appear above all PPC ads in local search results. The model is pay-per-lead (not pay-per-click), so budget risk is controlled. Ranking factors are star rating, review count, and answer speed — all areas where a responsive local operator can outperform a large chain. Get Google Guaranteed, hit 50+ reviews, and commit to sub-5-minute lead response. This is the single highest-ROI move available right now.
  • 2
    Build four geo-specific landing pages before running any PPC Immediate
    Mirror Precision's service-plus-city model. Build: /ac-repair-hiram-ga, /furnace-repair-hiram-ga, /ac-repair-dallas-ga, /furnace-repair-dallas-ga. Each needs city-specific copy, a phone number, and a clear CTA above the fold. Without these pages, every Google Ads dollar costs 30–50% more per click due to poor Quality Score. These pages also rank organically over time — giving you compounding returns.
  • 3
    Aggressively collect Google reviews — target 200+ in 90 days Immediate
    A post-job SMS with a direct Google Business Profile review link costs nothing. Review volume is a direct LSA ranking input and a trust signal that affects click-through rate across all organic, local pack, and paid placements. With 10+ years of loyal customers, Pro Air Tech has an untapped review base. A one-time review campaign to past customers can move the needle quickly.
  • 4
    Launch a maintenance membership plan High Priority
    A simple $19.99/month bi-annual tune-up plan builds ARR, improves cash flow predictability, and mirrors what Ragsdale and Precision are doing to maximize customer LTV. A member who pays $240/year is also statistically more likely to choose Pro Air Tech for repairs and replacements. This program also gives you a conversion hook for both ads and existing customer outreach.
  • 5
    Target E Dennis's customer base — they're vulnerable right now High Priority
    E Dennis went through an undisclosed partner merger in December 2025. Customers are filing BBB complaints about the change, and there's visible market confusion about the company's identity. A targeted local campaign in Cobb, Paulding, Douglas, and Cherokee counties with trust-forward messaging ("locally owned, same team, no surprises") could capture defecting E Dennis customers during this window.
  • 6
    Pursue manufacturer co-op certification (Carrier or Trane) Medium Term
    Ragsdale (Carrier President's Award) and Precision (Trane Comfort Specialist) are having a significant portion of their advertising subsidized through manufacturer co-op programs. Pro Air Tech competing at full cost against subsidized competitors is a structural disadvantage. Carrier and Trane certification also becomes a trust badge that can be used in ad copy, landing pages, and LSA profiles.
