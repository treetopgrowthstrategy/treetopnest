Your Insurance Carrier Doesn't Know
What Your Floor Is Doing

Most gym operators treat insurance as a fixed cost. Pay the premium. Hope nothing happens. Renew.

What most operators don't realize is that the risk profile their carrier is pricing was built around a version of their operation that doesn't exist anymore.

If you're running ecofit, your facility operates differently than the gym down the street. And the difference is measurable.

Where claims actually come from

Three categories drive most of the claims exposure in commercial fitness.

Equipment-related injuries are the highest cost per claim. Slip and fall incidents in wet areas and changing rooms are the highest frequency. And for unstaffed or 24-hour facilities, unauthorized access is a growing exposure that most standard policies weren't built to address.

In every case, the variable that matters most is not whether something goes wrong. It's how long the problem exists before someone catches it.

A treadmill with a developing fault will sit on your floor for weeks. Sometimes months. Waiting for a member to complain or a scheduled maintenance visit to find it. That window between the fault developing and someone acting on it is where claims live.

"The gap between when a fault develops and when someone acts on it is where operator liability accumulates."

What changes when you have floor intelligence

ecofit monitors every piece of equipment on your floor continuously. When a fault or abnormal pattern shows up, you know within days. Not weeks. For critical faults, our manufacturer partnerships push error codes directly from the equipment. The issues with the highest injury risk get flagged first.

That means you can show a complete, time-stamped record of equipment status across every site. You can demonstrate that equipment approaching end-of-life gets flagged before it fails. You can show that your highest-use machines get serviced more often because the data told you they needed it. Not because a calendar said so.

In plain terms, your operation shifts from reacting to problems after the fact to catching them before they become claims.

That's a different risk profile. The question is whether your carrier is pricing it that way.

The 1 to 2 percent case

I developed a white paper for insurance partners that lays out the full case. A 1 to 2 percent premium reduction for facilities running ecofit sits within the range that comparable risk-mitigation measures have historically supported. Think formal safety management systems or third-party inspections.

The difference is that ecofit runs continuously. It doesn't depend on someone remembering to do a walkthrough.

For operators who also run ecofit Vision AI, the case gets stronger. Vision AI monitors front desk coverage and staff check frequency in changing areas. Two high-risk zones that previously had no oversight. A facility that can prove consistent coverage in those areas is a better-managed facility. And it should be priced that way.
