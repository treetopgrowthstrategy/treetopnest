What we are solving for

Two objectives, one data informed route.

This assessment is built around the two priorities already on the table. Everything below is in service of reaching them with the least wasted effort.

Objective 01

Be the default result, including the AI answer

Rank in the compressed top of the page and earn citation inside the Gemini and AI Overview response. These are two related but distinct jobs. Ranking is classic SEO. AI citation rewards being a clean, structured, authoritative source. The plan resources both.

Objective 02

Do not elevate a small competitor

No direct comparison pages that hand a smaller player visibility. We validated this against the data, and the recommendation lands in the same place: focus the work on category demand, not on a competitor's name.

The strategy

Stay fitness first and still capture horizontal volume.

The tension is real. The big volume is horizontal and not fitness specific, yet ecofit should stay anchored in fitness. The resolution is a vertical wedge into a horizontal category, which is a defensible position rather than a compromise.

First

Own the intersection

Pages built on "asset management and predictive maintenance for fitness facilities" and "equipment analytics for gyms." Near zero competition, every visitor fully qualified, rankable in weeks.

→
Then

Take the soft head terms

Move into facility management, predictive maintenance, and equipment monitoring, the difficulty 5 terms first. Vision AI is the angle that sets the pages apart.

→
Finally

Qualify the spillover

Extend into asset tracking and asset management, framing the page toward equipment heavy, multi site operators. Gyms are the proof case, not the ceiling.

The honest tradeoff, stated plainly

Chasing the largest horizontal terms brings volume, but some of that traffic is non fitness and there is real competition at the top (Asset Panda, UpKeep, Limble, Fiix). Staying purely fitness keeps every visitor qualified but caps the ceiling at a few hundred visits a month.

The wedge threads it. Anchor the fitness intersection as the defensible core, start the horizontal expansion with the free money difficulty 5 terms, and let Vision AI carry the brand story across both.

Direct competitor comparisons

The data agrees: skip the head to head pages.

We checked whether there is any branded search demand worth capturing through a comparison page. There is not. Every competitor branded variant returns effectively nothing.

  • The qualified branded variants (competitor plus "fitness", "software", "gym", "pricing", "reviews") each return between 0 and 10 searches per month.
  • The bare brand term shows some volume, but it resolves to an unrelated climbing term and a separate AR software company, not commercial demand for this competitor.
  • A comparison page would therefore capture close to zero traffic while spending authority on a competitor's name.

The recommendation matches the stated objective exactly. Put the effort into category capture, where the demand actually lives.
