About

Rewritten

I've spent 25 years in sport and fitness, and I'll tell you what I keep seeing: operators are making million-dollar equipment decisions on gut feeling and guesswork. They walk the floor once a week, eyeball what looks busy, and hope their next buy is the right one.

That's the problem Ecofit was built to solve.

We give fitness facilities real-time visibility into what's actually happening on the floor. Not what members say they use. Not what the sales rep told you would be popular. What the equipment itself is telling you, every hour of every day. Utilization data. Performance trends. The difference between a cardio section that needs a refresh and one that's quietly running fine.

The fitness industry moves fast. New modalities. New brands. Members who upgrade their expectations every time they walk into a competitor's club. Staying on top of that moving target is hard enough without flying blind.

Ecofit has been adopted by some of the largest gym chains in North America because we give operators something they've never had before: one streamlined view of their entire equipment portfolio. Not a vendor portal. Not a spreadsheet. Actual intelligence, built for the way clubs run.

If you're responsible for equipment strategy, facility operations, or member experience at a fitness club, group, or chain, I'd like to connect. I've been in the buildings long enough to know what the data should actually tell you.

Most gym operators don't really know what's happening on their floor.

They know what they see when they walk through. They know what members complain about. They know which machines are always broken.

But here's the gap: none of that is actually data. It's observation. And in a business where a wrong equipment refresh costs $150K and five years of lease payments, observation isn't good enough anymore.

The clubs I'm talking to who are ahead of this problem have one thing in common: they've stopped guessing. They're looking at real utilization, real performance trends, real floor-level signal before they make any purchase decision.

That's what club intelligence looks like. Curious who else is thinking about this.
