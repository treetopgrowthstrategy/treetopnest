Smart Campaigns serve across Search, Search Partners, Display, Maps, Gmail, Discover, and YouTube — and crucially, the "keyword themes" you give the campaign act like very broad match: Google's automation decides which queries and which placements qualify. In this account, that automation has steered the vast majority of clicks to Google Display Network — historically the lowest-intent surface for HVAC repair searches. There is no "Display off" toggle inside a Smart Campaign; the fix is a campaign-type migration.

04.Findings — Ranked by Real Severity

CriticalRoot cause #1 — Only 4 verified completed phone calls in 30 days

For an HVAC repair business, phone calls are the dominant lead type. Across all call-tracking conversion actions, only 3 + 1 = 4 completed calls were attributed in the window, plus 8 tel: link clicks (not necessarily connected). At $6,312.59 spend, that is approximately $1,578 per verified completed call. Even if every form submission were real (and most are likely not — see next finding), the call volume alone is catastrophically low for the spend level.

Evidence Conversion-actions table, all "Call from Ads" sources, Apr 19 – May 18, 2026.

CriticalRoot cause #2 — 96% of reported "conversions" are GA4 page-view events on /contact-us

"Submit lead form" (369) and "Contact Us" (369) are identical counts, both GA4-imported, both labeled in the Ads UI as Page load: /contact-us. This pattern is consistent with the GA4 key events being configured to fire on a page view of the contact-us URL rather than on a verified form submission. If confirmed, the 738 "lead form" conversions reflect people landing on /contact-us — not people submitting it. The reported $8.22 CPA is then meaningless, and Smart Bidding is being trained on a non-signal. Must be verified in GA4 Admin → Key events before any further bidding decisions.

Evidence Conversion-action detail pages for ctId 7583879272 and ctId 7583580224.

CriticalRoot cause #3 — 94% of clicks and 78% of spend on Google Display Network

The Network segment confirms 7,271 of 7,771 clicks ($4,923 of $6,313 spend) come from Google Display Network impressions, not Google Search. Display traffic for HVAC repair is dominantly low-intent — people seeing banners on third-party sites, not someone whose A/C just broke. Only 122 clicks on Google Search in 30 days for an HVAC account at $329/day is the operative number.

The mechanism: Smart Campaign keyword themes function like broad match by design — Google's automation has wide latitude to match queries and select placements. Combined with the campaign's automatic Display expansion (which cannot be turned off inside a Smart Campaign), the budget gets pulled toward cheap Display impressions that "convert" easily against the page-view tracking. The two findings reinforce each other: high Display volume × page-view-based conversions = a healthy-looking dashboard built on noise.

05.Recommendations

Immediate (this week)

  • Verify the GA4 triggers. In GA4 → Admin → Events / Key events, confirm what conditions ads_conversion_Submit_lead_form_1 and ads_conversion_Contact_Us_1 actually fire on. If page-view-based, this is the single biggest finding and must be fixed before any other tuning.
  • Submit a test lead through the form on /contact-us/ and confirm with the client that it reached the intended recipient (email, CRM, dashboard). If it didn't, the "no leads" complaint is settled on the website side independent of Ads.
  • Confirm call-tracking number(s). Verify the tel: number on the call asset matches the verified business number and routes correctly during business hours. With only 4 calls in 30 days, even a small forwarding failure is material.
  • Pause one of the two duplicate "lead form" conversion actions (or set one to Secondary) so Smart Bidding stops double-counting the same event.
  • Investigate the Search Partners 25% CTR and file an invalid-traffic review with Google if appropriate.
  • Reconcile the 26-conversion gap with Google support if it doesn't self-resolve with date range adjustments.

Short-term (next 2–4 weeks)

  • Stand up Local Services Ads (LSA). The account is literally named "(LSA) Pro Air Tech," yet no LSA campaign exists. HVAC is one of the strongest LSA verticals — Google Guaranteed badging, top-of-page placement, pay-per-lead pricing. Given the catastrophically low call volume from the Smart Campaign, LSA should probably be the primary acquisition channel for this business, not a side experiment. Begin the verification and licensing process this week.
  • Migrate off Smart Campaign to a standard Search campaign. Smart Campaigns force-serve Display and don't let you exclude it. A standard Search campaign with proper match-type and placement controls would stop 78% of current spend from going to Display and redirect to actual HVAC search queries. (Note: do not request "turn Display off" inside the existing Smart Campaign — that toggle does not exist; migration is the only path.)
  • Rebuild conversions as real form submissions and connected calls, not page views. Either GA4 with a real form-submit event listener, or Google Ads native form-submit tracking with a thank-you-page trigger that only fires after submission.
  • Tighten geography. Center on Hiram and the realistic service radius; remove or de-emphasize Marietta/Dallas references unless the business genuinely services those evenly.
  • Fix the Podium widget or remove it from the contact page so visitors aren't bouncing off a broken chat affordance.

Medium-term (1–3 months)

  • Set up call-recording / call-tracking (CallRail or equivalent) so the agency and client can see what's happening on the few calls that do come in (intent, geography, missed calls, after-hours).
  • Add a thank-you / confirmation page after the form submission so a true conversion can be measured from a /thank-you/ pageview rather than from /contact-us/.
  • Once conversion tracking is trustworthy, set Smart Bidding to Maximize conversions with a target CPA aligned to actual booked-job economics — not the artificially low $8.22 today.
  • Reassess at 30 days post-migration. Expectation if migration succeeds: fewer reported "conversions" (because page-views stop counting), but higher real call and form-submit volume, and a much higher but truthful CPA. The dashboard will look worse on paper and the business will feel better in practice — manage that expectation with the client up front.
