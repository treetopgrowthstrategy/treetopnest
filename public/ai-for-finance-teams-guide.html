AI for Finance Teams: What Works, What Doesn't, and How to Deploy

Finance teams have more to gain from AI than almost any other function — and more to lose if it's deployed carelessly. Here's where the leverage is and where the guardrails are.

By Bill Colbert, founder of Treetop Growth Strategy · Updated June 2026

Finance workflows are disproportionately well-suited to AI assistance: they're document-heavy, formula-driven, and involve significant amounts of structured summarization and communication. The risk is equally disproportionate: errors in financial reporting have legal consequences, and AI systems that confidently produce wrong numbers are a specific category of dangerous.

The practical framework: use AI heavily for communication and summarization, cautiously for analysis and modeling, and never for final numbers without human review.

High-Value AI Applications in Finance

Board and investor reporting: AI excels at turning financial data into narrative. Give Claude a set of numbers and ask it to write the CFO commentary, the board update, or the investor letter. The output requires human review and accuracy verification, but the drafting time drops from hours to minutes. Most financial writing follows predictable patterns — AI learns those patterns faster than most humans do.

Variance analysis narration: Explaining why actuals deviated from budget is one of the most time-consuming finance tasks. AI can generate the first draft of variance commentary from data — "Revenue was $40K below plan due to delayed enterprise deal closures in Q3, partially offset by outperformance in SMB." The finance team verifies the causal attribution; AI handles the sentence construction.

Contract review and extraction: AI can review vendor contracts, subscription agreements, and customer contracts to extract key financial terms — payment schedules, price escalation clauses, cancellation terms, auto-renewal dates. Not a substitute for legal review, but a fast first pass that reduces time-to-extract on large contract libraries.

Expense report processing: AI can categorize expenses, flag policy violations, and draft the summary communication for employees whose reports require corrections. Low-risk, high-volume, well-suited to automation.

Scenario modeling narration: After you've built the financial model yourself, AI can write the plain-English explanation of each scenario for board consumption. "In the base case, we reach profitability in Q3 2026 assuming customer acquisition at $380 CAC and NRR of 112%."

Where to Be Careful

Never trust AI-generated numbers without verification: AI can generate plausible-looking financial figures that are completely wrong. Any number an AI produces that goes into a financial report must be verified against source data. This is not a limitation that will be solved in the near term — it's a fundamental characteristic of how these models work.

Revenue recognition: AI should not make revenue recognition decisions. The rules are complex, context-dependent, and have significant accounting and legal implications. AI can help you understand the rules; a CPA should apply them.

Tax calculations: Same principle. AI can summarize tax concepts and help draft communications about tax strategy, but tax calculations require human and professional review.

The Finance AI Stack

Claude for writing, summarization, and analysis narration. Excel or Google Sheets as the source of truth for numbers (don't let AI touch these directly). Zapier for automating recurring report generation workflows. DocuSign or PandaDoc for contract processing pipelines that include AI extraction steps.

How is AI used in finance?

AI is most valuable in finance for: board and investor report drafting (turning numbers into narrative), variance analysis commentary, contract term extraction, expense report categorization, and scenario modeling explanation. AI should be used cautiously for analysis and never for final numbers without human verification — financial errors have legal consequences.

Can AI automate financial reporting?

AI can automate the narrative and communication layers of financial reporting — CFO commentary, board updates, variance explanations — but not the underlying numbers. Financial data must be verified by humans against source systems. The automation value is in drafting time, not data generation.

What AI tools do finance teams use?

Finance teams most commonly use Claude or ChatGPT for writing and summarization, Excel or Google Sheets (unchanged) for financial modeling, Zapier for workflow automation, and specialized tools like Mosaic or Cube for FP&A that include AI features. Contract review AI tools include Luminance and Kira for large legal teams.
