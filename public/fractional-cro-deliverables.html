Fractional CRO Deliverables: What You Should Receive in 30, 60, and 90 Days

The most common failure mode in fractional CRO engagements is vague scope. Here's exactly what you should receive and when — and what to do if you're not getting it.

By Bill Colbert, founder of Treetop Growth Strategy · Updated June 2026

Fractional CRO engagements fail for one consistent reason: the scope is defined in terms of activities rather than deliverables. "Provide sales leadership" is an activity. "Deliver a documented sales playbook with ICP, qualification criteria, objection handling, and compensation structure" is a deliverable. The difference matters because activities are hard to evaluate and easy to claim credit for. Deliverables are either done or they're not.

Use this as a contract addendum or a performance framework when evaluating candidates.

Days 1-30 Deliverables

  • Revenue Audit Report: Written assessment of current state across sales, marketing, CS, and RevOps. Includes NRR, CAC, pipeline conversion rates, team capacity, and tech stack. Should be 8-12 pages with actual data, not impressions.
  • ICP Definition Document: Written ideal customer profile with firmographic criteria, technographic signals, behavioral indicators, and disqualification criteria. Must be agreed upon by sales and marketing.
  • Revenue Model: A working financial model showing current ARR, projected growth at current conversion rates, and the impact of specific interventions on the trajectory.
  • Priority Action List: The top 5 revenue interventions ranked by impact and effort, with owners and timelines. This is the roadmap the next 60 days executes against.

Days 31-60 Deliverables

  • Sales Playbook (v1): Qualification framework, discovery question library, objection handling guide, competitive differentiation, and closing process. Not a generic template — specific to your product, market, and motion.
  • MQL/SQL Agreement: A written, signed-off definition of what constitutes a Marketing Qualified Lead and a Sales Qualified Lead, agreed upon by both functions. The handoff process and SLA should be documented.
  • Pipeline Review Cadence: A running weekly pipeline review that the CRO runs with the sales team. Not a status update — a coaching and forecasting session with documented outcomes.
  • CS Expansion Playbook (v1): The trigger-based playbook for expansion conversations, including which customer signals indicate readiness, what the expansion offer is, and how success is measured.

Days 61-90 Deliverables

  • 90-Day Revenue Report: Baseline vs current on every metric from the Day 1 audit. What changed, what didn't, and why. Presented to leadership or the board.
  • RevOps Infrastructure: CRM configured with the correct pipeline stages, fields, and reporting. Attribution tracking that connects marketing activities to closed revenue.
  • Compensation Plan: Sales compensation structure aligned to company revenue goals, with quota design, accelerators, and spiff structure documented.
  • Months 4-6 Revenue Plan: The committed investment plan for the next phase, with specific programs, budget, and success metrics. The fractional CRO's next engagement should be defined by this document.

What's NOT in Scope for a Fractional CRO

Being clear about what a fractional CRO doesn't deliver prevents scope creep and disappointment:

  • Day-to-day sales rep management (that's a sales manager's role)
  • Writing individual sales emails or proposals
  • Running every customer call or demo
  • Building the product roadmap (that's a CPO)
  • Doing the work that should be done by the team they're leading

A fractional CRO who is spending most of their time doing individual contributor work has either taken on too much scope, or the team underneath them isn't capable of executing. Both are problems worth naming early.

What should a fractional CRO deliver in the first 30 days?

In the first 30 days, a fractional CRO should deliver: a revenue audit report with actual data across sales, marketing, CS, and RevOps; an ICP definition document agreed upon by both sales and marketing; a working revenue model showing current trajectory; and a prioritized action list for the next 60 days.

How do you measure a fractional CRO's performance?

Measure against specific deliverables with timelines (not activity descriptions), and against revenue metrics at 60 and 90 days. Key metrics: pipeline coverage ratio, MQL-to-SQL conversion, SQL-to-close rate, NRR, and CAC payback trend. A CRO who can't produce a written revenue audit by day 30 is behind schedule.

What is a reasonable timeline for a fractional CRO to show results?

Structural deliverables (playbooks, alignment documents, RevOps setup) should appear in days 30-60. Early revenue signal (improved conversion rates, healthier pipeline) should be visible by day 60-90. Meaningful revenue impact (closed deals attributed to new processes) typically appears in months 3-6. Anyone promising transformative revenue results in the first 30 days is overselling.
