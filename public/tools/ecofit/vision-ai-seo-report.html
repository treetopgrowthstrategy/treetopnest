Where the traffic is
Section 2

The plain-language terms that do work.

These are the clusters operators actually search. Volumes and difficulty are real Ahrefs US figures. We have flagged the two clusters where intent is a partial mismatch (people-counter hardware, and the Placer.ai-owned foot-traffic category) so we do not oversell them. The green clusters are the core play.

Cluster A · Occupancy and "how busy is my gym" ~900–1,000 / mo · KD 0–14

Member-facing and operator-facing "how busy" intent, almost all at difficulty 0. Carries AI Overviews on several terms, so it doubles as an AEO play. Terms: least busy gym times (300), least busy times at gym (150), gym busy times (40), gym crowd meter (20), how busy is the gym right now (20), gym occupancy (20), most busy gym times (20).

Best first targets: least busy gym times (300, KD 0) and gym crowd meter (20, KD 0). Angle: how operators surface real-time occupancy, funneling to Vision AI.

Cluster B · Gym operations and management software ~6,500–7,000 / mo · KD 1–60

The real B2B buyer cluster, and the highest commercial intent in the set (CPC $14 to $25). Head terms are owned by incumbents; the long tail is open. Terms: gym management software (1,500, KD 50), gym software (700, KD 51), best gym software (500, KD 58), gym software features (200, KD 8), small gym software (200, KD 12), gym software pricing (200, KD 1).

Best first targets: gym software features (200, KD 8), small gym software (200, KD 12), gym software pricing (200, KD 1). Skip the KD 50+ head terms near term.

Cluster C · Equipment maintenance and utilization ~800 / mo · KD 0–9

Directly on-message for the predictive-maintenance and equipment-health story, and the SERP is only local repair shops, so a national data angle can rank fast. Terms: gym equipment maintenance (250), equipment utilization software (150), fitness equipment maintenance (100), gym equipment maintenance services (80), gym equipment maintenance near me (70).

Best first targets: gym equipment maintenance (250, KD 0) and equipment utilization software (150, low competition).

Cluster D · People counting and occupancy sensing ~6,000 / mo · KD 0–11 · intent caveat

High volume, very low difficulty, but the intent is mostly wrong. "Occupancy sensor" (2,500) is lighting control (Lutron, Leviton). "People counter" (1,700) is retail and warehouse hardware (V-Count). Only the software slices fit. Treat as adjacency and awareness, not core.

If pursued: only people counter software (150, KD 2) or ai people counter (150). Do not chase the hardware head terms.

Cluster E · Foot traffic and location analytics ~4,500 / mo · KD 0–34 · Placer.ai owns it

Adjacent SaaS demand, low difficulty on the informational terms, but Placer.ai is the incumbent brand (11,000 volume on its own name) and the parent topic for most terms. We differentiate: Placer reads outside-the-building mobility data, ecofit reads inside-the-box equipment use. Terms: foot traffic analysis (400, KD 5), foot traffic data (300), foot traffic analytics (250), footfall data (150, KD 0).

Best first targets: foot traffic analysis (400, KD 5) and footfall data (150, KD 0) as informational feeders that pivot to in-club measurement.

Cluster F (smart home gym, ~14,000 volume) we deliberately skip. It is consumer D2C equipment shoppers (Tonal, Speediance, Aeke), not the ecofit operator buyer. Chasing it would bring traffic that never converts.

The second front
Section 4

The LLM-citation angle, while search catches up.

Traditional SEO in an emerging category is slow. The faster-moving opportunity is AEO: being the source that AI answer engines cite when someone asks about measuring gym equipment usage, in-club occupancy, or floor utilization. Several of our target terms already trigger AI Overviews in the SERP (least busy gym times, gym busy times, most busy gym times), which means the answer box is live and citable right now.

  • Own the definitional query. When someone asks an AI "how do gyms track which equipment gets used," we want ecofit to be the cited source. That means clear, structured, plain-language pages that state the answer directly, which is exactly what the pillar and FAQ are built to do.
  • The category name is an asset here. Because we are early to "equipment intelligence" and "Vision AI for gyms," we can define those terms cleanly and become the canonical explanation an LLM reaches for, before a crowded field of lookalikes muddies it.
  • The aged domain earns trust faster. A roughly ten-year-old domain is a credibility signal both to traditional ranking and to the models deciding whom to cite. We are not starting from zero authority.
  • This buys time. AEO wins can show up in weeks, not the six to twelve months organic ranking takes, so we get visible proof of the strategy working while the slower SEO build compounds underneath it.

Expectations
Section 5

Here is what realistic looks like.

No inflated promises. SEO in an emerging category is a slow, compounding build, and the honest version is more useful to Tim and Phil than a hockey-stick chart. Here is the timeline and what to expect at each stage.

Weeks 1–4

Publish the pillar and first two supporting pages. Win the AEO front.

Ship the Vision AI pillar plus the maintenance and occupancy supporting pages. Structure them for AI citation. Earliest AI Overview citations and long-tail impressions can appear here. No meaningful ranked traffic yet, and that is expected.

Months 2–3

Long-tail KD 0 terms start to rank.

The difficulty-0 targets (least busy gym times, gym equipment maintenance, gym software pricing, footfall data) begin surfacing on page one or two. First real clicks. Small numbers, right direction. This is where we prove the plain-language funnel converts to category pages.

Months 4–6

Cluster coverage compounds; internal links do their job.

With six to eight supporting pages live and linking to the pillar, the pillar starts ranking for its own broader terms and the supporting pages stabilize. Realistic organic in the low hundreds of monthly visits, heavily weighted to the maintenance and occupancy clusters.

Months 6–12

Authority accrues; mid-difficulty terms come into range.

As the domain earns links from the PR and partner placements already in the plan, KD 30–50 terms (foot traffic analysis, gym management long tail) become reachable. This is when the category itself may start showing early search volume, and we are already the definitive page for it.

The one-line expectation

Do not expect thousands of visits in the first quarter. Expect the right foundation. Realistic is a few hundred well-qualified operator visits a month by month six, an AEO citation footprint that shows up sooner, and ownership of the category vocabulary for the moment the market starts searching it. The volume is modest by design because the audience is narrow and high-value: operators making six-figure equipment decisions, not consumers shopping home gyms.
