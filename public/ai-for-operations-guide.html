AI for Operations: Automating the Workflows That Run Your Business

Operations is where AI creates the most durable leverage — not through flashy applications, but through systematic automation of the high-volume, structured workflows that consume your best operators' time.

By Bill Colbert, founder of Treetop Growth Strategy · Updated June 2026

Operations teams are responsible for making everything run — which means they spend significant time on tasks that are well-defined, repetitive, and procedurally intensive. These are exactly the tasks where AI delivers consistent leverage. The most effective operations AI deployments don't replace operational judgment — they eliminate the low-value execution overhead that consumes the time needed for high-value judgment.

Where AI Creates the Most Leverage in Operations

SOP and process documentation: Standard Operating Procedures are essential and chronically underdocumented in most companies. AI can draft SOPs from a recorded workflow walkthrough, a bullet-point description of the process, or an interview with the person who runs it. A complete SOP that would take a human 4 hours to write can be drafted in 20 minutes with AI — then reviewed and approved by the process owner.

Vendor management communications: Vendor onboarding documentation, contract review summaries, renewal communication, performance review templates — these are communication tasks that AI handles well. The COO or operations manager reviews and approves; AI does the drafting.

Meeting summaries and action item tracking: Operations teams often own the meeting cadence for the company. AI note-taking tools (Fathom, Otter, Fireflies) automatically generate meeting summaries and action items. AI can also draft the follow-up email to meeting participants with the agreed-upon action items and owners.

Data reconciliation reporting: Many operations workflows involve reconciling data from multiple systems — comparing what was sold vs what was delivered, what was budgeted vs what was spent, what was planned vs what actually happened. AI can generate the reconciliation summary and flag discrepancies for human review.

Job description and hiring documentation: Operations teams often own the hiring process workflow. AI drafts job descriptions, interview guides, offer letter templates, and onboarding checklists — reducing the time from "we need to hire" to "job posted" significantly.

The Operations AI Workflow Architecture

Most operations AI deployments follow the same pattern: a trigger (form submission, scheduled time, manual initiation), an AI processing step (Claude generates a document, summary, or communication), a human review step (the relevant operations owner approves or edits), and a distribution step (the approved output goes where it needs to go). Zapier handles the trigger-and-distribute layers; Claude handles the processing layer; Airtable tracks the workflow status.

Measuring AI Impact in Operations

The right metrics: hours saved per workflow per week, error rate on AI-assisted vs manual tasks, time-from-trigger-to-output on recurring deliverables. The most compelling metric for executive buy-in: what did this operations team member spend their time on instead of the task AI now handles?

How do operations teams use AI?

Operations teams use AI most effectively for: SOP and process documentation generation, vendor communication drafting, meeting summary and action item tracking, data reconciliation reporting, and hiring documentation. The highest-leverage applications are high-volume, well-defined tasks that currently consume significant time from experienced operators.

Can AI write SOPs?

Yes. AI can draft Standard Operating Procedures from a recorded walkthrough, a bullet-point process description, or an interview transcript. The draft requires review by the process owner, but reduces SOP creation time significantly. AI-generated SOPs are particularly useful for processes that exist in people's heads but have never been formally documented.

What is the best AI tool for business operations?

Claude (Anthropic) is the most versatile tool for operations AI tasks including document drafting, communication generation, and process summarization. Zapier connects Claude to other operational systems. Airtable provides the database layer for tracking workflow status. Fathom or Otter handle meeting intelligence. Together these tools cover most operations AI needs without requiring engineering support.
