✦ READY TO POST May 25, 2026
Podcast Reaction: What Al Mochavani Got Right — And Where Equipment Intelligence Goes Next
Thought Leadership
LinkedIn Post Copy — Dave Johnson
Caught the latest Lifts podcast with Al Mochavani and one framework stuck with me: Any technology you invest in should do at least 2 of these 3 things: → Enhance the member experience → Increase staff productivity → Contribute to the bottom line Simple filter. Most of what gets pitched to operators right now doesn't survive it. Here's what does — and where I think the next wave of equipment intelligence is going: We've done a solid job tracking cardio. Treadmill sessions, usage windows, error codes. That's table stakes now. The real gap? Everything else on the floor. Recovery areas. Functional fitness zones. Turf. Sauna. Cold plunge. These spaces represent significant capital and significant member time — and most operators have almost zero visibility into how they're being used. Who's using them? When? How long? Are those members retaining better? Layer in camera-based dwell time analysis and you start filling gaps that on-machine sensors can't reach. Which zones create friction. Where members congregate before they commit to a machine. Where the hidden bottlenecks are at peak hours. When you combine that with on-machine data, you stop reporting and start recommending. That's where EcoFit is headed. Total asset intelligence — not just cardio, not just the machines with sensors on them, but the full picture of how your facility is actually being used. Al said something else that landed: operators need to stop treating technology as an IT conversation and start treating it as an operations conversation. A retention conversation. A capital allocation conversation. Couldn't agree more. If you've ever wondered how much more you could know about your facility — Take our assessment → [LINK HERE]
