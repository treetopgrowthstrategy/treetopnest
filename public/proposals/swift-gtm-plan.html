The Problem with Most Agreements

A preferred vendor agreement that produces zero referrals isn't a partnership — it's a logo on a slide. The goal is 10+ activated partners, not 10+ signed agreements.

Rule: Go deep on 3–4 partners before spreading thin. A full co-marketing calendar with 3 great partners outperforms shallow agreements with 10.

Partnership Activation Playbook

  • Execute preferred vendor agreement with SLA on referral terms
  • Co-branded one-pager developed within 30 days
  • Briefing call with billing company's CSM team
  • Email introduction sent by billing company to their customer base
  • SWIFT listed in partner portal, marketplace, or newsletter
  • Launch webinar co-branded within 60 days
  • Quarterly joint pipeline review and co-marketing calendar
  • SPIFFs or referral fees for billing company reps who make warm intros

Pillar Three
05

Former Customer Win-Back

Why This Is the Warmest Pipeline
  • Already agreed to the commission-only model
  • Already experienced the service
  • Zero structural objection to re-engaging
  • Lapsed for operational reasons only — email decay, contact changes, CRM gaps
Tone

Re-introduction, not a pitch. "We've made some updates and want to reconnect" — not "come back." Personal where possible, sequenced where not.

Target Recovery Rate

20–30% reactivation on reachable contacts. Zero acquisition cost per account.

Win-Back Execution Plan

  • Audit all historical customer records — identify the lapsed cohort fully
  • Segment by reason for lapse: email bounce, contact change, unknown
  • For email-unreachable: attempt LinkedIn, phone, and billing company channels
  • Personalized re-engagement sequence: 5–6 touches over 3 weeks
  • Dedicated win-back landing page with updated brand positioning
  • Offer: priority onboarding, no waiting period — restart immediately
  • For high-value former accounts: direct outreach from SWIFT leadership
  • Track and measure: % of lapsed accounts re-engaged, reactivated, converted

Email Recovery Tactics

  • Apollo enrichment to find current owner/GM email
  • Cross-reference LinkedIn for contact changes
  • Check if gym migrated to a billing partner — use as warm path in
  • Physical direct mail as last resort for unreachable contacts

Re-Engagement Messaging

  • Lead with what's changed — new integrations, new partners
  • Include a hard result from a comparable gym
  • Anchor in outcomes, not features
  • Show SWIFT is meaningfully better than when they last engaged

High-Value Account Protocol

  • Flag accounts above a revenue threshold for personal outreach
  • Direct contact from SWIFT leadership — not a sequence
  • Priority onboarding offered: no waiting period, restart immediately
  • Track % re-engaged, reactivated, and converted by cohort

New Website & Digital Presence

One Job

Validate the decision a prospect is already leaning toward when they arrive from an outbound email or billing partner referral.

Positioning Requirements
  • Member reactivation — not collections, not billing software
  • Zero risk framing on every page
  • Performance-only model front and center
  • Case studies with hard dollar amounts by vertical
Primary CTA

"Get Your Free Recovery Estimate"

Not "Contact Us." Not "Request a Demo." The estimate is the first step in the sales conversation.

Site Architecture

  • Home: Hero with value prop, social proof strip, 3-step how-it-works, case study callouts, CTA
  • How It Works: Process detail, integration overview, what SWIFT does vs. what gym does
  • For Billing Partners: Partner-specific page with co-sell value prop and partnership inquiry form
  • Case Studies: 5–8 stories with real recovery numbers by vertical
  • ROI Calculator: Input gym size → estimated monthly recovery → commission breakdown
  • Get Started: Simple application form, no friction — name, gym, email, billing platform

Tech Recommendation

  • Platform: Webflow — custom-coded feel, no dev dependency for updates
  • CRM integration: Native form capture into HubSpot or custom CRM
  • Timeline: 45–60 days from kickoff to launch
  • Analytics: GA4 + Hotjar from day one

SEO Target Keywords

  • Gym member reactivation service
  • Failed draft recovery for gyms
  • Gym revenue recovery program
  • Commission-based member win-back
  • Fitness studio lapsed member recovery

Billing Partner Page

  • Dedicated page for billing company co-sell conversations
  • Partner-specific value prop (reduces customer churn for the platform)
  • Current partner logos displayed prominently
  • Partnership inquiry form — separate from gym inquiry

CRM Migration: HubSpot → Custom

Migration Window: Sept–Oct

Deliberate timing — not rushed. Moving mid-growth-push creates risk. Schema design starts now so the Q3 build isn't compressed.

Recommended Stack
  • Database layer: Airtable
  • CRM front-end: Softr or Stacker
  • Email sequences: Instantly (already live)
  • Enrichment: Apollo MCP integration
  • Automation: CoWork workflow triggers
Why Migrate at All
  • Owned infrastructure — no licensing dependency
  • Shaped around SWIFT's actual sales motion
  • Partner tracking, win-back cohorts, Mindbody segment — built in from day one

Required Modules

  • Contact & account management (gym operators, billing partner contacts)
  • Deal pipeline with stage tracking (prospecting → demo → live)
  • Email sequence management and activity logging
  • Billing partner tracking (partner account, referral volume, co-marketing status)
  • Former customer cohort tagging and win-back workflow
  • Reporting: pipeline velocity, conversion rates, revenue recovered per account
  • Mindbody account segment tracking

Migration Phases

  • Q2 (Now–June): Schema design and field mapping. HubSpot remains live — no disruption to active pipeline.
  • July–Aug: Airtable structure built. Front-end layer developed. Shadow testing against live HubSpot data.
  • Sept–Oct: Parallel run — both systems live simultaneously for 4–6 weeks. Validate zero data loss.
  • Oct–Nov: Full cutover. HubSpot decommissioned. New system becomes primary.

Data Migration Priorities

  • Active gym accounts and deal stages (no loss)
  • Billing partner contacts and relationship notes
  • Former customer records (tagged for win-back cohort)
  • Email sequence history and activity logs
  • Contact enrichment data from Apollo

Stack Recommendation

  • Database: Airtable (already in Bill's infrastructure)
  • Front-end: Softr or Stacker for CRM UI layer
  • Email: Instantly (already live) for sequence delivery
  • Enrichment: Apollo MCP integration
  • Automation: CoWork for workflow triggers
Pillar Six
08

Direct Outbound GTM Engine

When Direct Outbound Is the Path
  • Large-format traditional health clubs
  • Franchise groups not yet covered by a billing partner
  • Verticals outside Mindbody's core user base
  • Gyms in markets with no active billing partner presence
Stack Already in Place
  • Instantly — email deliverability, multi-domain
  • Apollo — contact enrichment
  • CoWork — research and personalization per account

ICP Scoring Criteria

  • 500+ active members (enough failed-draft volume to justify)
  • Monthly EFT/draft billing model (not pay-per-class only)
  • Multi-location preferred — one deal, multiple sites
  • Operator or GM is identifiable and reachable
  • Not yet active on SWIFT (not a current gym)
  • Not on a billing partner platform (to avoid channel conflict) or explicitly included with partner blessing

List Sources

  • IHRSA member directory (serious operators)
  • Franchise location rosters (Anytime Fitness, Crunch, Orangetheory, F45, CycleBar)
  • State business license databases (fitness/health club NAICS codes)
  • Google Maps scrapes by metro, filtered by review count (proxy for size)
  • Billing partner customer lists (with blessing)
  • Ahrefs backlink data from competitor and adjacent sites

Three Messaging Wedges

  • Math Wedge: "Gyms your size typically recover $X/month in lapsed draft. Pure performance — we only earn when you do."
  • Peer Wedge: "We're running member reactivation for [N] gyms in [their metro]. Here's what a comparable operator recovered last quarter."
  • Partner Wedge: "As a preferred vendor for [billing company they use], we're reaching their customer base about member win-back…" (highest conversion)

Sequence Cadence

  • 6–8 touches over 4 weeks
  • Email + LinkedIn + one phone touch (Day 10)
  • Trigger events: new location openings, hiring for retention/membership roles, negative review clusters
  • CoWork handles research and personalization per account
  • Instantly manages deliverability across multiple domains

Vertical expansion targets beyond traditional gyms: Martial arts academies, swim schools, dance studios, yoga chains, tennis clubs, and cycling boutiques all run recurring draft billing and experience the same lapsed-member problem. The pitch is identical. The verticalization of case studies and messaging is the only incremental work.

Direct Mail — Worth Testing

Physical Mail to Gym Operators

Email inboxes are crowded. A well-designed physical mailer to a gym owner's facility address cuts through in a way digital can't. Commission-only is a model that reads even better in print — no risk, nothing to lose.

  • Format: Oversized postcard or single-page letter — not a brochure
  • Targeting: ICP-qualified list (500+ member facilities, specific verticals)
  • Message: One sharp line, one number, one URL — no clutter
  • URL: Dedicated landing page with tracking to measure response rate
  • Cost: ~$1–2 per piece — justified if conversion rate exceeds 1%
  • Use case: Win-back for unreachable former customers; cold outreach to high-value targets
Live Events — FISA

Sal Pellegrino / FISA Events — Pre-Booked Sales Meetings

FISA events bring together fitness industry operators and vendors in a structured format with pre-booked one-on-one meetings. This is one of the highest-quality in-person channels available — you're sitting across from qualified decision-makers, not working a trade show booth.

  • Format: Pre-scheduled 1:1 meetings with gym operators/owners
  • Audience: Multi-location operators, chains, and franchise owners — SWIFT's ICP
  • Advantage: Face-to-face with a captive, pre-qualified audience — no cold approach needed
  • Prep: Personalized one-pagers per meeting, case study by vertical, leave-behind collateral
  • Follow-up: Same-day email recap + Instantly sequence triggered post-event
  • Action: Identify upcoming FISA calendar — secure meeting slots well in advance

Next 30 Days — What Gets Done First

A plan without prioritization is just a document. The following actions are ordered by leverage and dependency — complete these in sequence and the rest of the plan follows naturally.

  • 01

    Lock Messaging & Positioning Framework

    Define the exact language for "member reactivation vs. collections" across all channels. Write the one-sentence positioning statement, the three headline variations, and the FAQ responses to the most common objections. This is the foundation everything else is built on.

    Week 1
  • 02

    Audit the Lapsed Customer List

    Pull every former customer from HubSpot. Categorize by reason for lapse (email bounce, contact change, unknown). Identify the reachable cohort. This data shapes the win-back sequence and informs the CRM migration priorities simultaneously.

    Week 1
  • 03

    Initiate Website Brief & Designer Search

    Write the website creative brief: 6-page architecture, positioning language, CTA structure, and functionality requirements (ROI calculator, partner page, form capture). Begin designer or agency outreach. Webflow is the recommended platform.

    Week 1–2
  • 04

    Segment Mindbody Account Base

    Work with Mindbody to pull or access a filtered list of accounts by size, vertical, and geography. Identify the top 5,000 accounts by ICP score. Build the initial Instantly sequence specifically for Mindbody operators.

    Week 2
  • 05

    Launch Win-Back Sequence

    For every lapsed customer with a reachable email: send a personally framed re-engagement sequence. 5–6 touches, warm tone, lead with what's new. Apollo enrichment for any contacts where email has bounced. Don't wait for the new website — send from the team now.

    Week 2–3
  • 06

    Identify & Prioritize Next 3 Billing Partners

    From the target list (Jonas, Twin Oaks, Clubessential, Motionsoft, Zen Planner, Wodify, PushPress, EZFacility), score by customer base size and relationship warmth. Initiate outreach on the top 3 with a clear preferred vendor pitch and activation playbook attached.

    Week 2–3
  • 07

    Lock FISA Event Calendar & Secure Meeting Slots

    Identify the next Sal Pellegrino / FISA events on the calendar. Pre-booked 1:1 meetings are the highest-quality in-person channel available — but slots fill. Get on the calendar early. Prep materials: one-pager by vertical, leave-behind with a hard recovery number, and a post-event follow-up sequence ready to fire same day.

    Week 3
  • 08

    Build Case Study Pack

    Develop 5 customer case studies with real recovery numbers, one per major vertical. Format for website, email, and sales conversation use — including a one-page version for FISA meetings. These are the single most effective sales tool in the arsenal; every other channel benefits from having them.

    Week 3–4
  • 09

    Direct Mail Pilot — Former Customers & High-Value Targets

    Design and send a direct mail pilot to two groups: (1) former customers who are email-unreachable, and (2) 50–100 ICP-qualified cold targets in a high-priority market. One clean message, one number, one URL with tracking. Measure response rate over 3 weeks before scaling.

    Week 4
  • 10

    Begin CRM Schema Design — HubSpot Stays Live Until Sept–Oct

    No migration yet — HubSpot remains the system of record through summer. What gets done now is schema design: contact objects, deal stages, partner records, sequence tracking, and reporting fields. Doing this early means the Airtable build in Q3 is fast and clean, not rushed.

    Week 4
