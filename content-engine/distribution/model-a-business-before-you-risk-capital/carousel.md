Slide 1: Most people use AI to write faster. The higher-leverage move is to make it argue with you before you risk a dollar.

Slide 2: For two years I aimed it at the wrong problem. Draft this, summarize that. Faster, not smarter.

Slide 3: Then I put a real deal in front of it and asked it to be the skeptic. Rebuild the model. Question the revenue line.

Slide 4: Twenty minutes later the bet did not survive its own math. Cheaper to kill on a screen than in the market.

Slide 5: We skip modeling because it felt like a chore. That cost just collapsed. The math was never the bottleneck. The friction was.

Slide 6: Three questions that break most proformas. What ramp does this assume? Whose labor is priced at zero? What happens in the worst month?

Slide 7: The tool does the math. You own the assumptions: what ramp is real, what your time is worth, whether the market is open or only looks open.
