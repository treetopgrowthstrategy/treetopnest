1/ Most people use AI to write faster.

The higher-leverage move is the opposite: make it argue with you before you risk a dollar.

2/ For two years I aimed it at the wrong problem. Draft this, summarize that. Useful. It made me faster, not smarter.

3/ Then I put a real deal in front of it and asked it to be the skeptic.

Rebuild the model. Question the revenue line. Show me where this only works if three optimistic things all happen at once.

4/ Twenty minutes later I had a cleaner read on that business than two calls with the people selling it to me.

The bet did not survive its own math. Cheaper to kill on a screen than in the market.

5/ We skip this because modeling felt like a chore. An afternoon in a spreadsheet you half trusted. So we call a gut read "conviction."

That cost just collapsed. The math was never the bottleneck. The friction was.

6/ Three questions that break most proformas:

- What ramp does this assume?
- Whose labor is priced at zero?
- What happens in the worst month, not the average one?

7/ The tool does the math. You own the assumptions: what ramp is real, what your time is worth, whether the market is open or only looks open.

8/ Before you commit capital, make the bet argue for its life first.
