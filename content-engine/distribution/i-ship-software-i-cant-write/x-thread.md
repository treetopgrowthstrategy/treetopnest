1/ I ran a HubSpot partner agency for twelve years.

Then I built my own CRM to replace it. And I cannot write a line of code.

2/ For years I watched people buy HubSpot like a name. The brand, the story, as if the software would log in and do the work for them. It never does.

3/ The pricing was a ladder that only climbed. A starter tier that could barely do anything, then 800 a month, then 1,200, for tools they used a fraction of, on contracts they could not leave.

4/ At some point I stopped seeing a tool and started seeing a toll. So I built my own. Deal tracking, outbound, exactly what I needed. It replaced Pipedrive, HubSpot, and Instantly.

5/ The surprise: the hard part was never the code. It was the requirements. Knowing exactly what I wanted, writing it clearly enough for a capable model to build, and knowing it when I saw it.

6/ The cadence that works is not think, build, think, build. That is the dopamine loop. It is think, think, think. Check, check, check. Vet, vet, vet. Then build.

7/ Syntax got cheap. Specification and judgment got scarce.

8/ I did not learn to code. I learned to deploy intelligence, and own my stack instead of renting it.
