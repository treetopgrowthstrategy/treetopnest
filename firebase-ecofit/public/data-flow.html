01

The 30-second overview

The Ecofit website has two lead-capture entry points: the Facility Intelligence Assessment (an 8-question funnel that estimates revenue at risk) and the Request a Demo modal (a short contact form). Both submit to serverless functions running on Google Cloud. Those functions do three things in parallel: send a personalized email back to the lead, send a notification to the team, and write a row to the CRM database. The assessment also generates a custom HTML report and hosts it on a public URL that gets included in the lead's email.

Live system status

Domain: ecofitnetworks.com is live and serving production traffic from Firebase. SSL is valid. Assessment and demo forms both passed end-to-end tests on June 8, 2026.

Notifications: Verified working. Assessment submit → 2 emails fire (lead autoresponder + team notification) in under 4 seconds plus report generation. Demo submit → 2 emails fire in under 5 seconds.

03

Entry points on the website

Every page on the site can drive someone toward one of two conversions. Each one is a distinct lead type and triggers a distinct downstream flow.

Entry point 1

Facility Intelligence Assessment

An 8-question funnel at /assessment. The questions feed a risk model that estimates how much revenue and capex the operator is leaving on the table. The lead form sits between the score reveal and a personalized report download.

Conversion intent: Educational. Best fit for cold prospects who want to understand their exposure before talking to sales.
Entry point 2

Request a Demo

A modal launched from the "Request a Demo" buttons throughout the site. Captures name, work email, company, locations, and phone, then routes the lead to a Google Calendar booking link.

Conversion intent: High. Best fit for sales-qualified visitors who want to see the platform in action.

05

Layer 2: Cloud Functions (the server side)

Form submissions are processed by serverless functions running on Google Cloud Functions for Firebase. There's no always-on server. When a form posts, Google spins up a Node.js 20 runtime, executes the handler, and shuts down. You only pay for invocations and compute time used.

The two endpoints

01

ecofitAssessmentSubmit

Receives the assessment payload (name, email, company, title, phone, 8 answers, computed risk range). Builds a personalized HTML facility intelligence report, uploads the report to Firebase Storage, emails the lead with their report link, emails the Ecofit team with intake details, and logs the row to Airtable.

Endpoint: POST /api/ecofit-assessment-submit Avg duration: 3-5 seconds Memory: 512 MB Region: us-central1
02

ecofitDemoRequest

Receives the demo modal payload (name, email, company, locations, optional phone). Emails the lead with a Google Calendar booking link, emails the Ecofit team with intake details, and logs the row to Airtable. All three operations run in parallel using Promise.allSettled, so a slow CRM doesn't slow down the user-facing response.

Endpoint: POST /api/ecofit-demo-request Avg duration: 1-2 seconds Today posts to treetopgrowthstrategy.com — fix before handoff

Environment configuration

The functions read secrets and config from environment variables. None of these are committed to source code. They are managed in the Google Cloud Console or via the Firebase CLI.

06

Layer 3: The fan-out

Once a form submission lands in the function, four things happen in quick succession. Three of them are user-visible (the report, the lead's email, the team's email). The fourth is the CRM write.

03a · Build the report

The assessment handler builds a complete HTML document in memory: a personalized title, the lead's risk band, a list of which signals contributed to that band, industry benchmarks, and an accuracy disclaimer. It's roughly 11 KB of HTML, generated in under 200 milliseconds.

03b · Push the report to Firebase Storage

The HTML report is uploaded to a public bucket at gs://ecofit-website.firebasestorage.app/reports/{company-slug}-{date}.html. The URL is publicly readable (no auth required) and cacheable for one hour at the CDN edge. This URL is what gets embedded in the lead's email.

https://storage.googleapis.com/ecofit-website.firebasestorage.app/
  reports/ecofit-{slug}-2026-06-08.html

03c · Send transactional email via Resend

Resend is a developer-focused email API similar to SendGrid or Postmark, but simpler. Two emails fire in parallel:

  • The lead email — Sent from the verified sender address. Includes the lead's risk band, a CTA to view the full report, and a CTA to book a demo.
  • The team notification — Sent to BILL_NOTIFY_EMAIL. Includes all intake fields and the report URL, so anyone can immediately see what came in.

Resend handles SPF, DKIM, and DMARC alignment automatically. Bounce and complaint webhooks can be wired in later to feed deliverability data back into the CRM.

03d · Log to CRM (Airtable today)

Every submission also writes a row to the Treetop Database Airtable base, in the "Ecofit Assessments" table. Columns include name, email, company, title, phone, location count, risk range, computed risk, all 8 answer values, the report URL, and a submitted-at timestamp.

Note on the current setup

The Airtable base today is owned by Treetop Growth Strategy. This is convenient for testing but not the right long-term home. The clean handoff path is to either migrate to HubSpot (recommended, since Ecofit already uses it) or stand up a new Ecofit-owned Airtable base. Either is a one-day project. See section 09.

09

Moving infrastructure cost to Ecofit

Today, every paid service in this stack is on a Treetop Growth Strategy billing account. That made it easy to ship quickly, but the long-term right answer is for Ecofit to own and pay for the infrastructure that runs Ecofit's website and pipeline. Here is the cost picture and three handoff options to pick from.

What the stack actually costs to run

The numbers below assume modest production volume: roughly 100 assessment submissions, 100 demo requests, and 50,000 page views per month. Free tiers cover most of this at current scale.

Three options for offloading cost

Recommended
Option A

Full handoff

Ecofit creates its own Google Cloud, Resend, and CRM accounts. We migrate the project, the domain config, and the email sender domain. Treetop stays on as a build partner but Ecofit owns and pays for everything in its own name.

Time to complete: 1 to 2 days
Setup cost: $0
Ongoing cost: ~$0 to $50/mo
Best for: Long-term clarity, audit trail, vendor independence
Option B

Split billing

The Firebase project stays under Treetop ownership, but the Google Cloud billing account is switched to an Ecofit-owned credit card. Email and CRM accounts get reattached to Ecofit billing. Treetop continues to administer.

Time to complete: Half a day
Setup cost: $0
Ongoing cost: ~$0 to $50/mo
Best for: Speed. Avoids any project migration risk.
Option C

Pass-through invoice

Treetop keeps every account in its own name. Once a month, Treetop pulls a usage report and invoices Ecofit for actual infrastructure costs plus a small management fee. No accounts change hands.

Time to complete: None (status quo)
Setup cost: $0
Ongoing cost: ~$50 to $100/mo (infra plus fee)
Best for: Short-term simplicity if Ecofit is not ready to add vendors.
Recommendation

Option A. Ecofit ends up owning every account that has its name on it, and the bill goes straight to Ecofit's finance team with no middleman. At current volume the all-in cost is small enough that the bigger value is the clean audit trail.

The migration is mostly admin work: provisioning four accounts (Google Cloud, Resend, Airtable or HubSpot, Cloudflare or registrar if domain ownership transfers), pointing the existing Firebase project's billing at the new Google account, and swapping in Ecofit-controlled sender addresses.

10

Action items before a clean handoff

These are the items I would close before transferring full ownership to Ecofit. None of them block leads from flowing today, but each one is a loose end that should be tied up for the long-term setup.

01

Repoint the demo form at the ecofit domain

The demo modal currently posts to https://treetopgrowthstrategy.com/api/ecofit-demo-request. That works today because the function is deployed under both projects, but it means a Treetop outage would break the demo form. Update the home page JS to post to /api/ecofit-demo-request (relative path, same origin) and confirm the function is deployed under ecofit-website.

Estimated effort: 30 minutes
02

Add www as a Firebase Hosting custom domain

www.ecofitnetworks.com currently returns 404. Adding the www domain to Firebase Hosting and redirecting it to the apex is a 10-minute config change.

Estimated effort: 10 minutes
03

Swap the notification recipient and sender addresses

Today the team notification email goes to a Treetop address. Update the BILL_NOTIFY_EMAIL environment variable to whichever Ecofit address should own inbound leads (a shared inbox like leads@ecofitnetworks.com works well). Verify a sending domain at Resend so the autoresponder shows up as from an Ecofit address rather than a Treetop one.

Estimated effort: 1-2 hours including DNS records
04

Move the CRM destination to an Ecofit-owned home

Either migrate the Airtable base to an Ecofit Airtable workspace, or replace Airtable with HubSpot. If HubSpot, we add a few lines to the handler that upsert a contact and create a deal in the right pipeline. The lead-funnel doc already walks through the HubSpot integration path in detail.

Estimated effort: 2-4 hours for HubSpot path
05

Pick a billing path and execute it

Option A, B, or C from section 09. Once chosen, this is mostly a finance-team task: provision the account, run the billing transfer, and confirm the first invoice routes to the right address.

Estimated effort: Half a day to two days depending on option
